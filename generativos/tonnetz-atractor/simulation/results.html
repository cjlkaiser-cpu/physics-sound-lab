Observations:

  • Higher force = more transitions but lower adjacency

8. Conclusions

Key Findings

  • The chaotic pendulum system achieves 70% adjacency rate compared to 8% for random selection
  • Voice leading averages 0.65 semitones - confirming parsimonious motion
  • P transformations dominate (57%), creating modal oscillation patterns
  • Lower force/friction configurations maximize coherence (up to 89% adjacency)
  • The system produces non-repeating yet locally coherent progressions
Implications for the Paper

These quantitative results support the central thesis: chaotic dynamics constrained to the Tonnetz topology produce musically coherent output. The 70% adjacency rate demonstrates that the physical simulation naturally follows Neo-Riemannian voice-leading principles without explicit rules.

The dominance of P transformations suggests the system has an inherent tendency toward modal ambiguity - a characteristic valued in contemporary harmonic practice and ambient music.
